About Me

I am a Junior Group Leader for Biomedical Machine Learning at the MRC Biostatistics Unit, University of Cambridge. My research sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence, biostatistics, medical imaging, and theoretical physics. I develop novel generative models, explore spatio-temporal dynamics for forecasting, and apply geometric and mathematical principles to build robust AI systems for biomedical science.

Previously, I was a Research Scientist at the Computer Vision Lab, ETH Zurich, where I led projects on generative modelling, CT radiomics, MRI super-resolution, and quantum machine learning. I completed my PhD at ETH Zurich on fluid and quantum solvers in curved and topological manifolds, and my undergraduate and master's degrees in Physics at the University of Cambridge.

Research Interests

  • Spatio-temporal dynamics and forecasting
  • Medical imaging (Computed Tomography, Radiomics, Super-Resolution)
  • Biostatistics (Genomics, Epidemiology)
  • Generative modelling and AI (Gradient flows, Normalizing Flows, VAEs, Diffusion Models)
  • Differential geometry and theoretical physics
  • Fluid–structure interaction and Lattice Boltzmann methods
  • Quantum machine learning
